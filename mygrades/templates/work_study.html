Work Study Credit - 9th through 12th Grade Only

You are eligible to earn a full credit, each school year, if you have a job or volunteer at a business. This work takes the place of one elective each semester on your schedule. The work can be anything from babysitting to mowing yards, or even working at Sonic.

If you have a job or are helping at home, you can earn high school credit.

To earn credit, you must first let your teacher know. He or she must request your schedule be adjusted so that you get credit.

You will provide the following documentation FOR EACH SEMESTER THAT YOU RECEIVE 1/2 CREDIT:

  1. Log 60 hours per semester. You can set up a log in Google docs and share it with your teacher. This can be copies of pay stubs that show hours worked or even a log of time that is signed by a supervisor. Get this to your teacher. Sample Log.
  2. Submit the following three documents one time each school year:
    • Create both a resume and cover letter one time each school year, not one for each semester.
      Help With Resumes and Cover Letters for High School Students
    • Complete a research paper covering careers of interest including educational requirements and salary ranges. Just one per year, not one each semester. Follow these Career Research Paper Directions./li>
    • Write a Reflective Essay over the work study/volunteer/internship you did this semester. Use this guide.

Internship (Volunteer) - 9th through 12th Grade Only

You are eligible to earn a full credit, each school year, if you have a job or volunteer at a business. This work takes the place of one elective each semester on your schedule. The work can be anything from babysitting to mowing yards, or even working at Sonic.

If you are performing duties or volunteering anywhere in your community, you can earn high school credit. Some ideas for these opportunities are:

  • Intern at a business
  • Intern with the Epic Communications Team (Virtual and/or OKC)
  • Volunteer at your church
  • Volunteer at a local food pantry
  • Volunteer at a local library
  • Volunteer at a local animal shelter or zoo
  • Volunteer at a local YMCA
  • Volunteer at a local nursing home
  • Volunteer at a local museum
